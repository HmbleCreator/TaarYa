Stellar Biography

Sirius A is a main-sequence star of spectral type A0 or A1, paired with a faint white dwarf companion, Sirius B (spectral type DA2). The distance separating the two varies between 8.2 and 31.5 astronomical units as they orbit every 50 years.

Recent photometric analysis suggests Sirius A is metal-rich, with an iron abundance [Fe/H] = 0.50. The system is approximately 200–300 million years old. Originally, the system comprised two bluish stars; however, the more massive Sirius B consumed its resources and became a red giant before shedding its outer layers and collapsing into its current white dwarf state approximately 120 million years ago.

The system is moving closer to the Solar System, so its brightness will slowly increase over the next 60,000 years.
